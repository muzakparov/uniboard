Typical Accommodations

The College Board considers all reasonable requests for accommodations needed by students with documented disabilities. Available accommodations are not limited to those listed.

Examples of typical accommodations include:
  • Extended time
  • Computer use for essays
  • Extra and extended breaks
  • Reading and seeing accommodations 
  • Four-function calculator

If you don’t see what you’re looking for, view a list of other accommodations.

College Board Approval Required

Use of accommodations without College Board approval results in cancellation of test scores. Learn how to submit a request >
